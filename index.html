Biography

    Junchen is a 3rd-year Ph.D. student in Software Engineering at Tsinghua University, studying networks and systems in SUN Group under the guidance of Prof. Yuan He. His academic interests include Industrial Internet of Things, Wireless Sensing, Mobile and Ubiquitous Computing. Junchen is currently the student leader of Pavatar project and a member of the photography team of Tsinghua student art group.

    During the first three years of Junchen's Ph.D., he has published several papers on high-level international journals and conferences, e.g. IEEE Signal Processing Magazine, IEEE INFOCOM, IEEE ICPADS, IEEE ICII and etc., and won the best paper award of IEEE ICII 2018 and best student paper award of IEEE ICPADS 2017. Moreover, he has participated in 6 invention patents as the main inventor, two of which have passed the preliminary examination and four have entered the substantive examination.

Recent Projects

Industrial IoT for the Power Industry

Industrial IoT Digital Twin


Pavatar is a digital twin for Ultra-High-Voltage Convertor Stations (UHVCS), leveraging cutting-edge Industrial IoT techniques. The goal of Pavatar is to improve the efficiency of system management, to assist control decision and to save labor cost. The main components of Pavatar includes data visualization in virtual reality, intensive networked video analysis, intelligent wireless sensing and deep system diagnosis. Recently, our team has deployed the prototype of Pavatar in ShaoShan UHVCS at Hunan, China, to comprehensively monitor the operation of the world's first 300MVar water-cooling compensators. Visit Pavatar Website for more information about Pavatar!

  • (11/2018) Pavatar has been demonstrated in SenSys 2018 at Shenzhen, Guangzhou, China
  • (09/2018) A Survey of Industrial IoT and introduction of Pavatar project is published in IEEE Signal Processing Magazine
  • (02/2018) A prototype of Pavatar has been deployed in ShaoShan UHVCS at Hunan, China
  • (11/2016) Our team has collaborated with the State Grid Corporation of China to launch the Pavatar project.

    • Battery-Free Liquid Leakage Detection Systems

    Industrial IoT Wireless Sensing RFID


    In modern industry, liquid leakage detection is one of the most headache problems for inspection workers. We have developed two editions of battery-free liquid leakage detection systems with RFID technology. Leveraging the near-field coupling effect of adjacent CTOS RFID tags to generate a distinct signal pattern, our approaches can achieve high detection accuracy while keeping low-cost and easy-to-deploy features.

  • (04/2019) TwinLeak (Edition II) is published in proceedings of IEEE INFOCOM
  • (10/2018) TagLeak (Edition I) is published in proceedings of IEEE ICII

    Industrial IoT Wireless Sensor Networks Software-Defined Networking


    Pangu is an innovated software-defined architecture for wireless sensor networks, which enables centralized network control over underlying ad-hoc opportunistic routing structure by defining the forwarding role (sender or forwarder) of sensor nodes.
